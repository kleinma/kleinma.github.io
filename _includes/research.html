Research and Projects

A Deep Learning Approach to Robot Perception and SLAM

2015 to Present

My current research focuses on robust feature extraction from LIDAR, RGB, and RGBD data to aid in long term loop closures during Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM). I am using modern Deep Learning techniques, such as Generative Adversarial Networks, to extract robust features from large amounts of unlabeled data.

Autonomous Robotic Snowplow

2013 to Present

Led team to a 2nd place finish in the ION Autonomous Snowplow Competition. Focused on sensor fusion and localization techniques, including an Extended Kalman Filter and Ultra-Wideband sensors. Developed in C++/Python using the Robot Operating System (ROS) framework. Also, redesigned steering mechanism, actuated plow, and power system, and path planning and steering algorithms.

Neural Controller for Legged Locomotion

2011 to 2013

Built a robotic cockroach leg and neural controller to study biologically hypothesized neural control networks for joint coordination. Focus was on the dynamics of networks of simulated neurons, central pattern generators, and force feedback. Worked directly with biologists to hypothesize neural connections.
